3. Description:
Stage-discharge relations are developed using a flow meter to measure the discharge of a river or stream at different stages, or water levels. These measurements are taken at a streamgage, which is a site used for measuring and recording information about a body of water. The development of an accurate discharge rating requires numerous measurements at all ranges of stage and streamflow. This is because the relationship between stage and discharge can be impacted by various factors, including the shape, size, slope, and roughness of the channel. To account for these factors, it's important to take measurements at a wide range of stages and flow levels. Additionally, these relations must be continually checked against ongoing discharge measurements because stream channels are constantly changing due to erosion or deposition of streambed materials, seasonal vegetation growth, debris, or ice. A station control is used to eliminate any effects of downstream conditions on the flow. Station controls can be natural or constructed.
