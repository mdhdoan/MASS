 1. Keywords: Water hardening, green egg weights, fecundity, predictive regression equations, salmon spawning
   
2. Abstract: The use of individual and average water hardened green egg weights is essential for generating accurate fecundity values in fish hatcheries and predictive fecundity regression equations. This method allows for more precise estimates of potential egg deposition from adult salmon that spawn in designated channels, as demonstrated in the Reintroduction of Lower Columbia River Chum Salmon into Duncan Creek FFY 2008 to 2010 Summary Report (<https://pisces.bpa.gov/release/documents/documentviewer.aspx?doc=P123791>). The process involves the examination of green egg weights, which are sensitive to water hardness levels, providing valuable insights into fish reproductive success and overall population health.

3. Description: Water hardening refers to the exposure of green eggs (unfertilized) to specific water conditions before hatching. Researchers use individual and average water hardened green egg weights in fecundity calculations for fish spawned at a hatchery, as well as for predictive fecundity regression equations that generate potential egg deposition estimates for adults spawning in channels. This approach helps establish baseline data for evaluating the reproductive success and population dynamics of fish species such as salmon. The methodology emphasizes the need to consider water hardness levels, which impact green egg weights and have implications for fecundity estimation. Further details on this process can be found in the Reintroduction of Lower Columbia River Chum Salmon into Duncan Creek FFY 2008 to 2010 Summary Report (<https://pisces.bpa.gov/release/documents/documentviewer.aspx?doc=P123791>).

4. Target: The primary focus of this method is the generation and improvement of fecundity estimates for fish species, specifically salmon, through water hardening techniques and predictive regression equations based on green egg weights.

5. Constraints: Although this method offers valuable insights into fish reproductive success and population health, it may be subject to limitations associated with the reliance on specific water conditions and egg weight measurements for accurate fecundity estimation. Factors such as temperature variations, water quality, and sample size can influence the precision of results and thus should be carefully monitored and controlled during the process.
