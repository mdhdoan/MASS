2. Abstract:
Beach seining is a method used for capturing fish in various aquatic habitats, such as rivers, estuaries, and nearshore marine environments. This technique involves using a seine net that is pulled through the water to encircle fish, allowing for their collection and study. Beach seining can be an effective way to sample fish populations, particularly when they are concentrated in shallow waters with few obstructions. However, successful beach seining depends on various factors, including habitat characteristics, water conditions, fish behavior, time of year, net design, and sampling methods.
3. Description:
Beach seining is a method that involves using a long, weighted net to encircle fish in a particular area. The net is typically made of mesh and has lead weights sewn along the bottom edge to help it sink. The top edge of the net is held up by floats, allowing the net to form a wall-like structure when pulled through the water. This method can be used to capture a wide variety of fish species and is often used for sampling purposes.
