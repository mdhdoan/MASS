 1. Relevant keywords: Canopy Cover Estimation, Transects, Visual Estimates, CHaMP Method, Peck et al. Methods
2. Abstract: This text describes the methodology for estimating canopy cover, which is essential for understanding vegetation distribution and health in riparian areas. The approach utilizes visual estimates at predefined transects within a 10mx10m plot, following the CHaMP-Riparian Structure Method derived from Peck et al. (2001) EMAP methods. This method excludes its application for the 2011 CHaMP protocol.
3. Description: The method focuses on estimating canopy cover, which is the proportion of area covered by vegetation in a given plot. Five transects are established along the right and left banks within a 10mx10m plot at bankfull level. For each plot, the percent of areal coverage of the canopy (>5 m) is visually estimated. The final Percent Canopy Cover is an average of estimates from all plots, while Percent No Canopy Cover is calculated as 100-PercentCanopyCover. The methodology adheres to the CHaMP - Riparian Structure Method, which is based on Peck et al. (2001) methods used by the EMAP. However, this specific method is not applicable for the 2011 CHaMP protocol.
4. Target: The primary goal of this method is to accurately estimate canopy cover in riparian areas for ecological assessment and monitoring purposes.
5. Constraints: This method cannot be applied to the 2011 CHaMP protocol, although it follows the Peck et al. (2001) methods used by the EMAP. The method relies on visual estimates, which might introduce subjectivity and require skilled personnel for accurate results.
