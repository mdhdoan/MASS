2. Abstract:
The Bonneville Power Administration funds a long-term study conducted by academic and federal scientists to investigate the influence of coastal ecosystems on the recruitment variability of salmon in Pacific Northwest waters. The research currently focuses on examining the effects of biotic and abiotic environmental conditions on the early marine life history of coho and Chinook salmon, specifically within the Columbia River Plume. This area is crucial for juvenile salmon and higher trophic level predators due to its high salmonid abundance and susceptibility to human-induced factors.
