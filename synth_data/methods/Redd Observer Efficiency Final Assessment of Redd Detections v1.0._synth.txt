This method aims to improve the accuracy of redd count estimates by comparing best estimates (census counts) with observer counts, thereby assessing observer efficiency. By addressing detectability errors, this approach helps to uncover important trends, ensuring effective conservation and management strategies for salmonid species.

This process is essential for identifying and addressing potential errors resulting from factors such as timing of counts, redd characteristics, spawning habitat characteristics, spawner densities, and surveyor experience. By analyzing these discrepancies, this method seeks to enhance the accuracy of redd count estimates, ensuring that important trends in salmonid populations are not obscured. In doing so, it contributes significantly to the conservation and management efforts for these species by providing a clearer understanding of their abundance and distribution patterns.
