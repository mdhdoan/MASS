2. Abstract:
Migration is a crucial phase in the life cycle of salmonids, but it can be challenging due to the need to adapt to different environments, particularly the transition from freshwater to saltwater. Smoltification is a series of morphological, behavioral, and physiological changes that enable juvenile salmonids to survive in seawater. One important aspect of smoltification is the increase in gill Na+, K+ ATPase (NKA) enzyme activity, which facilitates osmoregulation by extruding NaCl across the gills. This allows for increased salinity tolerance and survival in the ocean environment. Studies have shown that hatchery-reared fish often have lower gill NKA activity than wild fish, which is associated with higher mortality rates during saltwater stress. Measuring NKA activity can help determine differences in osmoregulatory preparedness for saltwater entry between hatchery and natural origin salmonids, highlighting the importance of understanding this mechanism for improving the success of hatchery rearing practices.
