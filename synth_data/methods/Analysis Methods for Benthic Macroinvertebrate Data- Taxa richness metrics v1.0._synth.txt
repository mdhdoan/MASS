 1. Keywords: Benthic macroinvertebrates, water quality assessment, sampling methods, data analysis, biotic integrity index
2. Abstract: This text describes a method for assessing water quality using benthic macroinvertebrates as indicators. The necessity for this approach arises from the need to understand the impact of human activities on aquatic ecosystems and to establish a standardized protocol for sampling, identification, and analysis. By comparing collected data with reference conditions, this method provides an effective tool for evaluating biological integrity and stream health. It is not intended for assessing individual habitats or macroinvertebrates as a food source but rather as a general-purpose tool for water quality assessment and comparison across different sites.
3. Description: The text begins by introducing benthic macroinvertebrates, their relevance to watershed health, and the importance of standardized sampling and analysis methods. The suggested field method ID 244 and laboratory method ID 164 are presented as the preferred approach for data collection.
The document emphasizes the use of reference conditions in the analysis process, allowing for comparison with expected conditions. Three major categories of data analyses are described: individual metrics, biological indices (such as IBI), and predictive models (e.g., RIVPACS). Each type of analysis is appropriate for different scenarios depending on capabilities, objectives, available data, and reference condition tools used.
Individual metric analysis involves evaluating specific characteristics of macroinvertebrate assemblages, with calculated values compared to reference condition data. While simple and easily interpreted, this method is not suitable for more regulatory decision-making processes.
4. Target: The primary focus of the described method is on assessing water quality using benthic macroinvertebrates as indicators by comparing collected data to reference conditions.
