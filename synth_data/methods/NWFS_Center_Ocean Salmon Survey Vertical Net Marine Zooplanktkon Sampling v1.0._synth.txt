2. Abstract:
The Bonneville Power Administration funds a long-term research project to understand coastal ecosystems and the factors influencing recruitment variability of salmon in the Pacific Northwest waters. This study focuses on examining biotic and abiotic environmental conditions' effects on coho and Chinook salmon early marine life history, primarily within the Columbia River Plume. The research highlights the area's importance due to high salmonid abundance and its susceptibility to anthropogenic factors, benefiting both juvenile salmon and higher trophic level predators.
