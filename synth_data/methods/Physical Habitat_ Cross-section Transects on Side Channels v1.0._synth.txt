 1. Keywords: Environmental Monitoring, Assessment Program (EMAP), wadable streams, data collection, stream side channels.

2. Abstract: This method is derived from an Environmental Monitoring and Assessment Program (EMAP) protocol for monitoring western wadable streams. The need for this method arises from the importance of regularly assessing the health of these water bodies to ensure their sustainability and the welfare of the ecosystems they support. By establishing criteria for performing data collection activities at stream side channels, this method enables more effective and standardized environmental monitoring, leading to improved management and conservation strategies for wadable streams.

3. Description: The Environmental Monitoring and Assessment Program (EMAP) protocol is adapted to focus on wadable streams in the western region. This methodology highlights the significance of conducting data collection activities at stream side channels, which serve as essential indicators of a stream's overall health. By closely following this EMAP-based approach, researchers can ensure consistency and comparability in their assessments, facilitating more accurate evaluations of wadable streams over time and across different geographical locations.

4. Target: The primary goal of this method is to establish standardized criteria for monitoring and assessing the health of western wadable streams through data collection at stream side channels.
